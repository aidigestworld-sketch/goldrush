## 1. You are interacting with an AI system

GoldRush's chat-based founder intake, and the business opportunity analysis it produces,
are generated using artificial intelligence — specifically large language models
(currently hosted via NVIDIA NIM) combined with an automated research and evidence-
evaluation pipeline. No human reviews or edits your individual analysis before you
receive it.

**[CONFIRM]** Art. 50(1) requires this to be clear "unless it is obvious from the
circumstances" — given GoldRush's whole premise is AI-generated analysis (the landing
page already says this), a lawyer may conclude it's already sufficiently obvious, but
having an explicit statement is the safer choice and costs little.

## 2. How the analysis is generated

1. You describe your business vertical and complete a founder profile interview.
2. Our system searches public sources (market data, industry reports, customer reviews,
   competitor information) related to your vertical.
3. AI models evaluate this evidence to identify a market gap, generate a hypothesis about
   it, and validate that hypothesis against further real-world evidence.
4. If — and only if — the evidence meaningfully supports it, the system produces a
   business opportunity recommendation with cited reasoning.
5. If the evidence doesn't clear our internal quality bar, you receive an honest
   "Insufficient Evidence" result rather than a manufactured recommendation.

## 3. Limitations you should know about

- The AI can misinterpret evidence, cite sources incorrectly, or reach a conclusion that
  doesn't hold up in practice.
- The evidence base is only as current as the public sources our system searched at the
  time of your run — market conditions can change quickly.
- This is a research aid, not a guarantee of business success, and not financial,
  investment, or legal advice.

## 4. Your data and the AI system

Your founder profile answers are used as input to the AI system to personalize your
analysis. See our [Privacy Policy] for details on what's collected and how it's used, and
which third parties (including the AI providers) receive it.
